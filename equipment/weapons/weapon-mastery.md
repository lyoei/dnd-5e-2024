# Weapon Mastery

Weapon Mastery is a **brand-new system** introduced in the 2024 Player's Handbook. Each weapon has a mastery property that provides an additional tactical effect when used by a character who has unlocked it.

## How Weapon Mastery Works

- Each weapon in the Equipment chapter lists a **Mastery** property.
- To use a weapon's mastery property, you must have a feature that **unlocks** it (such as the Weapon Mastery class feature).
- The number of mastery properties you can learn is specified by your class.
- You can use the mastery property every turn when making an attack with that weapon.
- The **Weapon Master feat** also allows any class to unlock a weapon's mastery property.

## Mastery Properties

### Cleave

> If you hit a creature with a **melee attack roll** using this weapon, you can make a melee attack roll with the weapon against a **second creature within 5 feet of the first** that is also within your reach. On a hit, the second creature takes the weapon's damage, but **don't add your ability modifier** to that damage unless that modifier is negative. You can make this extra attack only **once per turn**.

**Weapons with Cleave:** Greataxe, Halberd

**Tactical Use:** Excellent for crowd control when enemies cluster together. The second attack is essentially free, though it deals reduced damage (no ability modifier). Pairs well with Great Weapon Master and high-damage dice weapons.

---

### Graze

> If your attack roll with this weapon **misses** a creature, you can deal damage to that creature equal to the **ability modifier** you used to make the attack roll. This damage is the same type dealt by the weapon, and the damage can be increased only by increasing the ability modifier.

**Weapons with Graze:** Glaive, Greatsword

**Tactical Use:** Ensures you always deal at least some damage, even on a miss. Particularly strong with high Strength modifiers. Note that bonus damage from features like Rage or magic weapons does NOT add to Graze damage — only the base ability modifier counts.

---

### Nick

> When you make the extra attack of the **Light property**, you can make it as part of the **Attack action** instead of as a Bonus Action. You can make this extra attack only **once per turn**.

**Weapons with Nick:** Dagger, Light Hammer, Sickle, Scimitar

**Tactical Use:** This is extremely powerful because it frees up your Bonus Action for other uses (Cunning Action, spellcasting, etc.) while still getting two-weapon fighting attacks. A Rogue with a Scimitar (Nick) + Shortsword can attack twice without spending their Bonus Action.

---

### Push

> If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can **push the creature up to 10 feet straight away** from yourself if it is **Large or smaller**.

**Weapons with Push:** Greatclub, Pike, Warhammer, Heavy Crossbow

**Tactical Use:** Great for battlefield control. Push enemies off ledges, out of melee range of allies, into hazardous terrain, or away from objectives. No save or check required — it just happens on a hit. Size limit of Large prevents it from being universally applicable.

---

### Sap

> If you hit a creature with this weapon, that creature has **Disadvantage on its next attack roll** before the start of your next turn.

**Weapons with Sap:** Mace, Spear, Flail, Longsword, Morningstar, War Pick

**Tactical Use:** A defensive mastery property. Hitting an enemy effectively weakens their next attack. Excellent for tanks and front-line fighters who want to reduce incoming damage. Stacks well with Shield and high AC builds since the enemy attacks at Disadvantage.

---

### Slow

> If you hit a creature with this weapon and **deal damage** to it, you can reduce its **Speed by 10 feet** until the start of your next turn. If the creature is hit more than once by weapons that have this property, the **Speed reduction doesn't exceed 10 feet**.

**Weapons with Slow:** Club, Javelin, Light Crossbow, Sling, Whip, Longbow, Musket

**Tactical Use:** Useful for kiting tactics and preventing enemies from closing distance. The 10-foot cap means multiple Slow hits don't stack, but it still meaningfully reduces mobility. Best on ranged weapons where you want to keep enemies at a distance.

---

### Topple

> If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a **Constitution saving throw** (DC 8 + the ability modifier used for the attack roll + your Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, the creature has the **Prone condition**.

**Weapons with Topple:** Quarterstaff, Battleaxe, Lance, Maul, Trident

**Tactical Use:** Knocking enemies Prone grants Advantage on melee attack rolls against them (within 5 feet) and penalizes their attacks. Extremely powerful for melee-heavy parties. The Constitution save means it's less reliable against tough monsters, but when it works, it's devastating. Quarterstaff having Topple makes it a surprisingly strong Simple weapon choice.

---

### Vex

> If you hit a creature with this weapon and **deal damage** to the creature, you have **Advantage on your next attack roll** against that creature before the end of your next turn.

**Weapons with Vex:** Handaxe, Dart, Shortbow, Rapier, Shortsword, Blowgun, Hand Crossbow, Pistol

**Tactical Use:** The most generally useful mastery property. Landing one hit sets up Advantage for your next attack, creating a "hit streak" effect. Exceptionally good for Rogues (guarantees Sneak Attack on the follow-up) and any character who relies on landing hits consistently. Works on ranged and melee weapons alike.

## Classes with Weapon Mastery

The following classes gain the Weapon Mastery feature:

- **Barbarian** — Learns 2 mastery properties at level 1, increasing later
- **Fighter** — Learns 3 mastery properties at level 1, the most of any class, increasing later
- **Monk** — Gains access through martial arts weapons
- **Paladin** — Learns 2 mastery properties at level 1
- **Ranger** — Learns 2 mastery properties at level 1
- **Rogue** — Learns 2 mastery properties at level 1

Other classes can access Weapon Mastery through the **Weapon Master feat**.

## 2024 Changes

- **Weapon Mastery is entirely new.** It did not exist in the 2014 PHB. This is the single biggest mechanical addition to the equipment/combat system in the 2024 revision.
- In 2014, all weapons of the same damage die were essentially interchangeable. Mastery properties now give each weapon a distinct tactical identity.
- Two-weapon fighting was previously a fighting style + Bonus Action system. Nick fundamentally changes how dual-wielding works by freeing the Bonus Action.
- The system is designed to make martial classes more interesting in combat compared to spellcasters, addressing a long-standing balance concern.
