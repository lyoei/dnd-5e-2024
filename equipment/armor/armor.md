# Armor

The Armor table lists the game's main armor. Each type falls into a category: Light, Medium, or Heavy. The category determines how long it takes to don or doff the armor.

## Armor Training

Anyone can don armor or hold a Shield, but only those with training can use them effectively:

- **Light, Medium, or Heavy Armor:** If you wear armor and lack training with it, you have Disadvantage on any D20 Test that involves Strength or Dexterity, and you can't cast spells.
- **Shield:** You gain the AC benefit of a Shield only if you have training with it.

## Rules

- A creature can wear only **one suit of armor** at a time and wield only **one Shield** at a time.
- **Strength Requirement:** If the armor has a Strength requirement and your Strength score is lower, your speed is reduced by 10 feet.

## 2024 Changes

Key changes from the 2014 PHB:

- **Don/Doff times by category:** The 2024 PHB standardizes don/doff times by armor category (Light: 1 min; Medium: 5 min don/1 min doff; Heavy: 10 min don/5 min doff) rather than listing individual times per armor.
- **Shield don/doff:** Shields now use a **Utilize action** to don or doff (previously just an action). This aligns with the new Utilize action framework.
- **Armor Training terminology:** The 2024 PHB uses "Armor Training" instead of "Armor Proficiency" in some contexts, though both terms appear.
- **Heavy property for weapons (related):** The old "Small creatures have Disadvantage with Heavy weapons" rule was replaced with a Strength/Dexterity threshold, which is related to the armor Strength requirement concept.
- **Armor table itself:** The actual armor types, AC values, and costs are **largely unchanged** from 2014. The mechanical stats are the same.
