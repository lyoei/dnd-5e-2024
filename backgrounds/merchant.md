# Merchant

**Source:** Player's Handbook (2024)

## Overview

You were involved in the world of commerce, whether as a traveling trader, a shopkeeper, or a member of a trading company. You know how to evaluate goods, negotiate deals, and manage logistics. You've traveled trade routes, dealt with suppliers and customers alike, and learned that fortune favors the bold — and the lucky.

## Mechanical Benefits

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Ability Scores** | Constitution, Intelligence, Charisma (choose +2/+1 or +1/+1/+1) |
| **Skill Proficiencies** | Animal Handling, Persuasion |
| **Tool Proficiency** | Navigator's Tools |
| **Origin Feat** | Lucky |

## Equipment

Choose A or B:
- **(A)** Navigator's Tools, 2 Pouches, Traveler's Clothes, 22 GP
- **(B)** 50 GP

## Origin Feat: Lucky

You gain the following benefits:

- **Luck Points.** You have a number of Luck Points equal to your Proficiency Bonus and can spend them on the benefits below. You regain your expended Luck Points when you finish a Long Rest.
- **Advantage.** When you roll a d20 for a D20 Test, you can spend 1 Luck Point to give yourself Advantage on the roll.
- **Disadvantage.** When a creature rolls a d20 for an attack roll against you, you can spend 1 Luck Point to impose Disadvantage on that roll.

## 2024 Changes

- **New Background:** Merchant is new to the 2024 PHB. It did not exist in the 2014 PHB (Guild Merchant, a variant of Guild Artisan, was the closest equivalent).
