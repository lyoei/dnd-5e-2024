# Chapter 3: DM's Toolbox — Condensed Reference

The DM's Toolbox is an alphabetical miscellany of tools for prepping and running games. This condensed reference covers the most mechanically useful sections.

---

## 1. Chases

### Running a Chase
- **Initiative:** All participants roll Initiative at the chase start.
- **Movement:** On each turn, a participant can Dash for free (no action required). Additional Dashes follow normal rules (action to Dash).
- **Duration:** A chase lasts a maximum of **10 rounds** (1 minute). After that, the quarry escapes or the pursuers catch up.

### Escape Threshold
- At the end of each round, a fleeing creature that is **far enough ahead** (DM's discretion based on mapping) can attempt to escape.
- The fleeing creature makes a **DC 15 Dexterity (Stealth)** check to hide and escape; total cover or heavy obscurement may grant Advantage.

### Chase Complications (d20)
Roll at the start of each participant's turn:

| d20 | Urban Complication | Wilderness Complication |
|-----|-------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Large obstacle (DC 10 DEX save or fall Prone) | Thick brush (DC 10 STR check or Speed halved this turn) |
| 2 | Crowd blocks path (DC 10 STR/DEX to navigate) | Uneven ground (DC 10 DEX save or fall Prone) |
| 3 | Window/fence (DC 10 Athletics/Acrobatics to cross) | Low-hanging branch (DC 10 DEX save or 1d4 Bludgeoning) |
| 4 | Barrier forces detour (lose 10 ft. of movement) | Stream/ditch (DC 10 Athletics to cross or Speed halved) |
| 5–10 | No complication | No complication |
| 11–20 | No complication | No complication |

> [NEEDS VERIFICATION] The exact 2024 complications table may differ from above; verify against the printed DMG.

### Mapping Chases
- Use a **linear track** of spaces; each space = 10 feet.
- Participants start at positions based on the triggering event.

## 2. Curses and Magical Contagions

### Curse Types

| Type | Description | Removal |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Bestow Curse** | Spell-based; 1 min (level 3) to permanent (level 9) | *Remove Curse* |
| **Cursed Creatures** | Origin-based (e.g., werewolves, mummies) | DM decides how *Remove Curse* interacts |
| **Cursed Magic Items** | Deliberate or supernatural creation | Per item description |
| **Narrative Curses** | Violating taboos (breaking vows, defiling tombs) | Symbolically right the wrong; *Remove Curse* may only suppress |
| **Environmental Curses** | Location-based (desecrated sites, Abyssal rifts) | Leave the area + magical removal |

### Demonic Possession (Environmental Curse)
- **Trigger:** Interact with demonic objects or linger in desecrated locations.
- **Save:** DC 15 CHA or be possessed.
- **Effect:** When possessed creature rolls a **1 on a D20 Test**, the demon takes control. End of each turn: DC 15 CHA save to regain control.
- **Recovery:** DC 15 CHA save after each Long Rest. Also ended by *Dispel Evil and Good* or curse removal.

### Magical Contagions
- **Recuperation:** 3 days of complete rest → DC 15 CON save → Advantage on saves vs. the contagion for 24 hours.
- **Example contagions** include Cackle Fever, Sight Rot, and others with varying DCs and effects.

### Designing Curses/Contagions
- Benchmark: *Bestow Curse* equivalence (1 min = level 3, until dispelled = level 9).
- Narrative curses should have a **discoverable cure** tied to the transgression.
- Contagions should have a **transmission vector** and **escalating symptoms**.

## 3. Environmental Effects

### Extreme Cold
- **Temperature:** 0°F (−18°C) or lower.
- **Effect:** At the end of each hour, creatures without cold resistance/immunity or cold weather gear make a **DC 10 CON save** or gain **1 level of Exhaustion**.
- Creatures with natural cold adaptation (e.g., Goliaths) or cold weather clothing are immune.

### Extreme Heat
- **Temperature:** 100°F (38°C) or higher.
- **Effect:** At the end of each hour, creatures without heat resistance or water make a **DC 5 CON save** (DC increases by 1 for each subsequent hour) or gain **1 level of Exhaustion**.
- Creatures in heavy armor or heavy clothing have Disadvantage.

### High Altitude
- **Above 10,000 feet:** Creatures must make a **CON save** or gain Exhaustion (for each 6 hours of travel without acclimation).
- **Acclimation:** 30 days at the altitude.
- Creatures naturally adapted to altitude (e.g., Aarakocra) are immune.

### Strong Wind
- Disadvantage on ranged weapon attacks and Perception checks relying on hearing.
- Extinguishes open flames and disperses fog/gas.

### Heavy Precipitation
- Lightly obscures visibility. Heavy rain/snow = Disadvantage on Perception checks.
- Extinguishes open flames.

### Frigid Water
- Immersion in frigid water: **DC 10 CON save** each minute or gain 1 level of Exhaustion.
- Creatures with cold resistance/immunity automatically succeed.

### Underwater
- **Visibility:** Clear water = 60 ft.; murky = 10 ft.
- **Combat:** Melee weapon attacks have Disadvantage unless the weapon is a dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident.
- Ranged weapon attacks automatically miss beyond normal range and have Disadvantage at normal range (unless crossbow, net, or thrown javelin/spear).
- Creatures without a swim speed or *Freedom of Movement*: halved speed (unless using Dash).
- **Suffocation:** See PHB rules. Breathing creatures can hold breath for 1 + CON modifier minutes (min. 30 seconds).

## 4. Hazards

Each hazard is rated as **Nuisance** or **Deadly** for characters of certain level ranges.

### Example Hazards

| Hazard | Severity | Mechanics |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Brown Mold** | Nuisance (1–4) | 10-ft. patch. Creature starting turn within 5 ft.: **11 (2d10) Cold damage**. Fire damage causes it to expand. Cold damage destroys it. |
| **Fireball Fungus** | Deadly (1–4) | Small mushroom, luminous orange cap. When reduced to 0 HP: **explodes as *Fireball*** (8d6 Fire, DC 15 DEX save). Chain reaction possible with multiple nearby fungi. |
| **Green Slime** | Nuisance (1–4) | Drops from ceiling. **5 (1d10) Acid damage** per turn. Destroys wood/metal on contact. Killed by sunlight, *Cure Disease*, or freezing. |
| **Inferno** | Deadly (5–10) | Large fire area. Creature entering/starting turn: fire damage based on intensity. |
| **Poisonous Gas** | Varies | Cloud area. CON save or Poisoned + damage. |
| **Quicksand Pit** | Nuisance (1–4) | Creature sinks 1d4+1 feet per round. DC 10 STR check to move 5 ft. toward edge. Fully submerged = suffocating. |
| **Razorvine** | Nuisance (1–4) | 10-ft. square. Moving through: **5 (1d10) Slashing damage** per 5 ft. |
| **River Styx** | Deadly (11–16) | Contact: DC 15 INT save or *Feeblemind* effect. |
| **Rockslide** | Deadly (5–10) | DEX save or Bludgeoning damage + buried. |
| **Vicious Vine** | Nuisance (5–10) | Animated plant. Grapples creatures. |
| **Webs** | Nuisance (1–4) | Difficult terrain. DC 12 DEX save or Restrained. Flammable. |
| **Yellow Mold** | Nuisance (1–4) | Touched/disturbed: 5-ft. Emanation of spores. **DC 15 CON save** or **11 (2d10) Poison damage** + Poisoned for 1 minute. |

> [NEEDS VERIFICATION] Exact damage values and DCs for Inferno, Rockslide, Vicious Vine, and Poisonous Gas should be verified against the printed DMG.

## 5. Poison

### Poison Types

| Type | Delivery | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Contact** | Smeared on object; touched with exposed skin | Remains until touched or washed off |
| **Ingested** | Swallowed (food/liquid) | Must consume full dose; partial dose = reduced effect |
| **Inhaled** | Breathed in (5-ft. Cube) | Holding breath is ineffective |
| **Injury** | Applied to weapon/ammo (Bonus Action) | Triggered by Piercing or Slashing damage |

### Sample Poisons

| Poison | Type | Cost | DC | Effect |
|--------|------|------|----|--------|
| **Assassin's Blood** | Ingested | 150 GP | 10 | 6 (1d12) Poison dmg + Poisoned 24h; half on save |
| **Burnt Othur Fumes** | Inhaled | 500 GP | 13 | 10 (3d6) Poison dmg; repeat save each turn, 3 (1d6) on fail; 3 saves end it |
| **Carrion Crawler Mucus** | Contact | 200 GP | 13 | Poisoned + Paralyzed 1 min; repeat save each turn end |
| **Essence of Ether** | Inhaled | 300 GP | 15 | Poisoned + Unconscious 8h; wakes on damage |
| **Lolth's Sting** | Injury | 200 GP | 13 | Poisoned 1h; fail by 5+ = also Unconscious |
| **Malice** | Inhaled | 250 GP | 15 | Poisoned + Blinded 1h |
| **Midnight Tears** | Ingested | 1,500 GP | 17 | No effect until midnight; 31 (9d6) Poison dmg; half on save |
| **Oil of Taggit** | Contact | 400 GP | 13 | Poisoned + Unconscious 24h; wakes on damage |
| **Pale Tincture** | Ingested | 250 GP | 16 | 3 (1d6) Poison dmg + Poisoned; repeat save every 24h; damage can't be healed while Poisoned; 7 saves end it |
| **Purple Worm Poison** | Injury | 2,000 GP | 21 | 35 (10d6) Poison dmg; half on save |
| **Serpent Venom** | Injury | 200 GP | 11 | 10 (3d6) Poison dmg; half on save |
| **Torpor** | Ingested | 600 GP | 15 | Poisoned 4d6 hours; Speed halved |
| **Truth Serum** | Ingested | 150 GP | 11 | Poisoned 1h; can't knowingly lie |
| **Wyvern Poison** | Injury | 1,200 GP | 14 | 24 (7d6) Poison dmg; half on save |

All saves are **CON saves** unless otherwise noted.

### Harvesting Poison
- **Action:** 1d6 minutes from dead or Incapacitated venomous creature.
- **Check:** DC 20 INT (Nature) with Poisoner's Kit.
- **Fail by 5+:** Harvester is subjected to the creature's poison.
- **Yield:** 1 dose per creature.

## 6. Traps

### Simple Traps
Triggered once, then must be reset. Each trap has:
- **Trigger** — what sets it off
- **Effect** — what happens
- **Countermeasures** — how to detect/disarm

### Complex Traps
Act on Initiative each round. Have multiple components. Typically 3 successful checks to disable one element.

### Trap DCs and Attack Bonuses

| Trap Severity | Save DC | Attack Bonus |
|--------------|---------|-------------|
| Moderate | 10 | +5 |
| Dangerous | 15 | +8 |
| Deadly | 20 | +12 |

### Damage Severity by Level

| Character Level | Moderate | Dangerous | Deadly |
|----------------|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1–4 | 5 (1d10) | 11 (2d10) | 22 (4d10) |
| 5–10 | 11 (2d10) | 22 (4d10) | 55 (10d10) |
| 11–16 | 22 (4d10) | 55 (10d10) | 99 (18d10) |
| 17–20 | 55 (10d10) | 99 (18d10) | 132 (24d10) |

> [NEEDS VERIFICATION] The 2024 DMG may use updated damage values compared to the UA/2014 table above. The structure (3 severity tiers × 4 level bands) is confirmed for 2024.

### Example Simple Traps

**Falling Net**
- **Trigger:** Stepping on hidden pressure plate.
- **Effect:** Net falls, creature is Restrained. DC 10 STR check to free self/others. Net AC 10, 20 HP.
- **Countermeasures:** DC 10 WIS (Perception) to spot; DC 10 DEX (Sleight of Hand) with Thieves' Tools to disarm.

**Pit Trap**
- **Trigger:** Stepping on hidden pit cover.
- **Effect:** DC 10 DEX save or fall 10 ft. for 3 (1d6) Bludgeoning damage.
- **Scaling:** Deeper pits deal more damage. Add spikes for +3 (1d6) Piercing.

**Poison Needle**
- **Trigger:** Opening a lock/container without disarming.
- **Effect:** 1 Piercing damage + 3 (1d6) Poison damage. Can scale Poison damage: 7 (2d6) at levels 5–10, 14 (4d6) at levels 11–16, 24 (7d6) at levels 17–20.

**Collapsing Roof** [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Trigger:** Tripwire or weight plate.
- **Effect:** Bludgeoning damage (scales by level) in area. DEX save for half.

## 7. NPC Creation

### Quick NPC Generation
Roll or choose from these tables to quickly define an NPC:

**Appearance (d20):** Distinctive jewelry, piercings, formal clothing, ragged clothing, pronounced scar, missing teeth, missing fingers, unusual eye color, tattoos, birthmark, unusual skin color, bald, braided beard/hair, unusual hair color, nervous eye twitch, distinctive nose shape, distinctive posture, exceptionally beautiful, exceptionally ugly, obvious disease/deformity.

**Personality Trait (d20):** Argumentative, arrogant, blustering, curious, friendly, honest, hot-tempered, irritable, ponderous, quiet, rude, suspicious, etc.

**Ideal (d6):** 1 = Beauty, 2 = Charity, 3 = Greater Good, 4 = Knowledge, 5 = Power, 6 = Self-improvement (align to NPC's alignment for nuance).

**Bond (d10):** Dedicated to fulfilling a personal goal, protective of close family, protective of colleagues, loyal to a benefactor/patron, captivated by a romantic interest, drawn to a special place, protective of a sentimental keepsake, protective of a valuable possession, out for revenge, etc.

**Flaw/Secret (d12):** Forbidden love, enjoys decadent pleasures, arrogance, envies another creature's possessions, overwhelming greed, prone to rage, has a powerful enemy, specific phobia, shameful/scandalous history, secret crime, possession of forbidden lore, foolhardy bravery.

## 8. Doors

### Door Statistics (2024 DMG)

| Door Type | AC | HP | Break DC (STR Athletics) |
|-----------|-----|-----|--------------------------|
| **Glass** | 13 | 4 | 10 |
| **Wood** | 15 | 18 | 15 |
| **Stone** | 17 | 40 | 20 |
| **Metal (Iron)** | 19 | 72 | 25 |

- **Larger doors** (Large size): HP ×2 or ×3, Break DC +5.

### Lock Quality

| Lock Quality | Pick DC | Pick Time |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| Inferior | 10 | 1 Action |
| Good | 15 | 1 Action (simple) / 1 Minute (complex) |
| Superior | 20 | 1 Minute |

- Requires **Thieves' Tools**. Roll DEX (Sleight of Hand).
- Proficient in both Thieves' Tools + Sleight of Hand = **Advantage**.

### Secret Doors

| Concealment | Perception DC |
|------------|--------------|
| Barely hidden | 10 |
| Standard | 15 |
| Well-hidden | 20 |

- **Search action:** WIS (Perception) check covers a 10-ft. square.

### Portcullis

| Size | Iron DC | Wood DC |
|------|---------|---------|
| Medium (8′×5′) | 20 | 15 |
| Large (10′×10′) | 25 | 20 |
| Huge (20′×15′) | 30 | 25 |

- STR (Athletics) check as Utilize action to lift.
- Targets within 5 ft. have **¾ cover (+5 AC)** from attacks through the portcullis.

### Object AC by Substance

| Substance | AC |
|-----------|----|
| Cloth, paper, rope | 11 |
| Crystal, glass, ice | 13 |
| Wood, bone | 15 |
| Stone | 17 |
| Iron, steel | 19 |

## 9. Settlements

### Settlement Size Categories

| Category | Population | Typical Features |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Thorp** | 20–80 | A few farms, maybe a shrine |
| **Hamlet** | 81–400 | A couple of shops, an inn |
| **Village** | 401–900 | Several shops, a temple, constabulary |
| **Small Town** | 901–2,000 | Marketplace, inn, temple, town hall |
| **Large Town** | 2,001–5,000 | Multiple temples, guilds, fortifications |
| **Small City** | 5,001–12,000 | City walls, multiple districts, government |
| **Large City** | 12,001–25,000 | Major trade hub, university/academy, military |
| **Metropolis** | 25,001+ | Seat of power, multiple guilds, diverse population |

> [NEEDS VERIFICATION] The 2024 DMG settlement categories may differ from the above (which draws from historical 5e guidance). The 2024 DMG uses random tables for settlement features.

### Settlement Random Tables (2024 DMG)

Use these tables to quickly generate a settlement's character:

**Defining Feature (d20):** Sprawling cemetery, extensive gardens, grand temple, great library, huge marketplace, major port, massive fortress, renowned academy, etc.

**Claim to Fame (d20):** Artisans, delicious cuisine, excellent wine/ale, haunted location, key trade hub, powerful spellcasters, etc.

**Current Calamity (d20):** Monster infestation, plague/disease, political unrest, natural disaster, crime wave, etc.

**Local Leader:** Roll or choose an NPC type and their situation (beloved, illegitimate, incompetent, etc.).

**Notable Tavern:** Generate a name using "The [Adjective] [Noun]" format from the tables.

**Shop Types:** Roll to determine what specialty shops exist (apothecary, blacksmith, fletcher, jeweler, mason, etc.).
