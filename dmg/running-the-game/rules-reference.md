# DMG Chapter 2: Running the Game — Quick Reference

---

## 1. Difficulty Class (DC) Table

### RAW Summary

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — "Difficulty Class" table.*

| Task Difficulty | DC |
|---|---|
| Very Easy | 5 |
| Easy | 10 |
| Medium | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Very Hard | 25 |
| Nearly Impossible | 30 |

Only call for a roll when failure has meaningful consequences.

### DM Notes

- DC 5 = almost anyone succeeds; use sparingly or skip the roll
- DC 10 = competent characters succeed most of the time
- DC 15 = the default "standard challenge" DC
- DC 20 = requires expertise or luck
- DC 25 = only highly specialized characters can attempt
- DC 30 = legendary feats; a level 20 character with +11 needs a 19+

## 2. Improvising Damage

### RAW Summary

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — "Improvising Damage" table.*

| Dice | Examples |
|---|---|
| 1d10 | Burned by coals, hit by a falling bookcase, pricked by a poison needle |
| 2d10 | Struck by lightning, stumbling into a fire pit |
| 4d10 | Hit by falling rubble in a collapsing tunnel, stumbling into a vat of acid |
| 10d10 | Crushed by compacting walls, hit by whirling steel blades, wading through lava |
| 18d10 | Submerged in lava, hit by a crashing flying fortress |
| 24d10 | Tumbling into a vortex of fire on the Elemental Plane of Fire, crushed in the jaws of a godlike creature |

### RAW Summary — Damage Severity and Level

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — "Damage Severity and Level" table.*

| Character Level | Nuisance | Deadly |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | 1d10 | 2d10 |
| 5–10 | 2d10 | 4d10 |
| 11–16 | 4d10 | 10d10 |
| 17–20 | 10d10 | 18d10 |

- **Nuisance:** Stings but won't kill a healthy character
- **Deadly:** Could drop a character to 0 HP; use for serious threats

## 3. Advantage & Disadvantage

### RAW Summary

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — Advantage/Disadvantage guidance.*

- Multiple sources of advantage don't stack — still one extra d20
- Advantage and disadvantage cancel each other regardless of number of sources

### DM Notes

**Grant Advantage when:** circumstances strongly favor the character, creative positioning, Help action, or environmental aid.

**Impose Disadvantage when:** circumstances hinder the character, environmental impediment, or a condition imposes it.

Prefer advantage/disadvantage over ad-hoc modifiers — simpler and faster.

## 4. Running Social Interaction

### RAW Summary

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 & 4 — NPC Attitudes, Initial Attitude table.*

NPCs have one of three starting attitudes: **Friendly**, **Indifferent**, or **Hostile**.

| Attitude | Description | Persuasion DC |
|---|---|---|
| **Friendly** | Disposed to help | DC 0–10 for bigger asks |
| **Indifferent** | No strong feelings (default for strangers) | DC 10–20 |
| **Hostile** | Opposes the party | DC 20+ or impossible without leverage |

**Initial Attitude (d12):** 4 or lower = Hostile, 5–8 = Indifferent, 9+ = Friendly.

### DM Notes

- Roleplay first, roll second — let the conversation drive; call Charisma checks at key turning points
- Shift attitudes through play: gifts → Friendly; insults → Indifferent/Hostile
- A natural 20 on a social check doesn't mind-control NPCs
- Not everything is Persuasion — Intimidation works but has consequences; Deception bypasses attitude but risks exposure

## 5. Running Exploration

### RAW Summary

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — Travel rules.*

**Travel Pace:**

| Pace | Distance/Hour | Distance/Day | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fast | 4 miles | 30 miles | −5 penalty to passive Perception |
| Normal | 3 miles | 24 miles | — |
| Slow | 2 miles | 18 miles | Can use Stealth |

**Survival Needs:** 1 lb food + 1 gallon water/day (2 gallons in hot weather).

### DM Notes

- **Navigation:** Easy terrain = no check. Difficult terrain (forest/swamp/mountains) = DC 15 Survival. Maps/guides grant advantage or negate the check.
- **Foraging:** DC 15 Survival (DC 10 abundant, DC 20 barren) to find food/water for one day.
- **Tracking:** Wisdom (Survival), DC varies by terrain and conditions.
- **Noticing Threats:** Passive Perception vs. stealth/trap DCs.

## 6. Running Combat

### RAW Summary

*Source: DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — Running Combat. Verified against D&D Beyond.*

**Initiative:** 1d20 + Dexterity modifier (+ any feature bonuses). Ties: DM decides, or higher modifier goes first.

**Initiative Scores (optional):** 10 + all Initiative modifiers. Advantage on Initiative = +5 to score. Disadvantage = −5.

**Heroic Inspiration (2024 rule):** When a player rolls a **natural 1** on a d20 Test, they gain Heroic Inspiration (free advantage on any future d20 Test). One at a time. DM can also award it for great roleplay.

### Table-Ready Procedure

1. Determine if Initiative is needed (DM decides when combat starts)
2. Roll or use Initiative scores
3. Track Initiative with hidden list, open list, or index cards
4. Track monster HP (subtract or add damage totals)
5. Mark conditions on cards/tokens
6. Describe hits/misses cinematically
7. End early if outcome is obvious — narrate the conclusion

### DM Notes

- Describe the environment shifting during combat (tables breaking, fires spreading)
- Use all five senses in narration
- Let players narrate killing blows
- "Bloodied" at half HP is a useful narrative signal
- For mobs: determine how many attackers hit based on target AC vs. attack bonus, skip individual rolls

## 7. Character Advancement

### RAW Summary

*Source: PHB 2024 — XP Thresholds table. DMG 2024, Chapter 2 — Advancement guidance.*

**XP Thresholds:**

| Level | XP | Level | XP |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | 11 | 85,000 |
| 2 | 300 | 12 | 100,000 |
| 3 | 900 | 13 | 120,000 |
| 4 | 2,700 | 14 | 140,000 |
| 5 | 6,500 | 15 | 165,000 |
| 6 | 14,000 | 16 | 195,000 |
| 7 | 23,000 | 17 | 225,000 |
| 8 | 34,000 | 18 | 265,000 |
| 9 | 48,000 | 19 | 305,000 |
| 10 | 64,000 | 20 | 355,000 |

**XP Awards:** Divide total monster XP equally among party members (including helpful NPCs).

**Milestone (alternative):** PCs level up at story milestones. Major milestone = high-difficulty encounter XP. Minor milestone = low-difficulty.

### DM Notes

- **Session-Based pacing:** Level 2 after session 1, level 3 after session 2, level 4 after 2 more sessions. Then 2-3 sessions per level. Above 10, speed up to 1-2 sessions per level.
- **Variant — Training to Level:** Requires downtime + GP. Level 2-4: 10 days/20 GP. Level 5-10: 20 days/40 GP. Level 11-16: 30 days/60 GP. Level 17-20: 40 days/80 GP.
- **When to level up:** Some DMs allow immediate leveling; others wait for Long Rest or session end. Both are fine.
