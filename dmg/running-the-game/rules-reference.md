# DMG Chapter 2: Running the Game — Quick Reference

## 1. Difficulty Class (DC) Table

Use this when setting DCs for ability checks. Only call for a roll when failure has meaningful consequences.

| Task Difficulty    | DC  |
|--------------------|-----|
| Very Easy          | 5   |
| Easy               | 10  |
| Medium             | 15  |
| Hard               | 20  |
| Very Hard          | 25  |
| Nearly Impossible  | 30  |

**Guidelines:**
- DC 5 = almost anyone succeeds; use sparingly or skip the roll
- DC 10 = competent characters succeed most of the time
- DC 15 = the default "standard challenge" DC
- DC 20 = requires expertise or luck
- DC 25 = only highly specialized characters can attempt
- DC 30 = legendary feats; a level 20 character with +11 needs a 19+

## 3. Damage Severity and Level

Use this to calibrate environmental/trap damage relative to character level.

| Character Level | Nuisance | Deadly |
|-----------------|----------|--------|
| 1–4             | 1d10     | 2d10   |
| 5–10            | 2d10     | 4d10   |
| 11–16           | 4d10     | 10d10  |
| 17–20           | 10d10    | 18d10  |

- **Nuisance:** Stings but won't kill a healthy character
- **Deadly:** Could drop a character to 0 HP; use for serious threats

## 4. Advantage & Disadvantage Guidelines

### Grant Advantage When:
- Circumstances strongly favor the character (e.g., attacking a restrained creature with a melee attack)
- The character has creative, clever positioning or approach
- An ally uses the Help action
- Environmental factors clearly aid the attempt

### Impose Disadvantage When:
- Circumstances hinder the character (e.g., shooting at a prone creature from range)
- Environmental factors impede the attempt (fog, slippery floor)
- The character is under a condition that imposes it (Frightened attacking source, Poisoned on ability checks)

### Key Principles:
- Multiple sources of advantage don't stack — it's still one extra d20
- Advantage and disadvantage cancel each other regardless of number of sources
- Prefer advantage/disadvantage over ad-hoc modifiers; it's simpler and faster

## 5. Running Social Interaction

### NPC Attitude System

NPCs have one of three starting attitudes toward the party:

| Attitude      | Description                                           | Persuasion Effect                       |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Friendly**  | Disposed to help; wants good things for the party     | Will help at low/no risk; DC 0–10 for bigger asks |
| **Indifferent**| No strong feelings; default for strangers             | Might help if it benefits them; DC 10–20 |
| **Hostile**   | Opposes the party; wants them to fail or suffer       | Won't help willingly; DC 20+ or impossible without leverage |

### DM Tips:
- **Determine starting attitude** based on faction, history, and context
- **Roleplay first, roll second** — let the conversation drive the scene; only call for Charisma checks at key turning points
- **Shift attitudes** through play: a gift might move Indifferent → Friendly; an insult might shift Friendly → Indifferent
- **Insight checks** reveal an NPC's attitude, desires, or whether they're lying
- **Not everything is Persuasion** — Intimidation works on Hostile NPCs but may have consequences; Deception bypasses attitude but risks exposure
- A natural 20 on a social check doesn't mind-control NPCs — it's the best possible outcome within reason

## 6. Running Exploration

### Travel Pace

| Pace   | Distance/Hour | Distance/Day | Effect                                    |
|--------|---------------|--------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Fast   | 4 miles       | 30 miles     | −5 penalty to passive Perception          |
| Normal | 3 miles       | 24 miles     | —                                         |
| Slow   | 2 miles       | 18 miles     | Can use Stealth                           |

### Navigation
- In easy terrain (roads, open plains): no check needed
- In difficult terrain (dense forest, swamp, mountains): DC 15 Wisdom (Survival) to avoid getting lost
- **Getting Lost:** The party veers off course; DM secretly determines new direction. They may realize they're lost with a subsequent Survival check
- **Maps and Guides:** A good map or local guide grants advantage on navigation checks or negates the need entirely

### Foraging
- DC 15 Wisdom (Survival) to find food and water for one day
- Abundant lands (forest, coast): DC 10
- Barren lands (desert, arctic): DC 20
- A character needs 1 pound of food and 1 gallon of water per day (2 gallons in hot weather)

### Other Exploration Activities:
- **Tracking:** Wisdom (Survival), DC varies by terrain and conditions
- **Noticing Threats:** Passive Perception vs. stealth/trap DCs
- **Searching:** Investigation or Perception for hidden objects, secret doors

## 7. Running Combat

### Initiative
- Each creature rolls **1d20 + Dexterity modifier** (+ proficiency if they have a relevant feature)
- 2024 rules: the DM may also allow **1d20 + Wisdom modifier** for characters with certain features [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- Ties: DM decides, or the creature with the higher modifier goes first

### Tracking HP
- **Use average HP** for monsters for speed (listed in stat block)
- For large groups ("mobs"), consider using the **Mob Attack table** (DMG p. 82): determine how many attackers hit based on d20 needed, skip individual rolls
- Track monster HP loosely if preferred — describe wounds narratively ("bloodied" at half HP is a popular convention)

### Narration Tips
- Describe hits and misses cinematically — don't just say "you hit for 8 damage"
- Let players narrate their killing blows
- Describe the environment shifting during combat (tables breaking, fires spreading)
- Use all five senses: sounds of clashing metal, smell of sulfur, etc.

### Heroic Inspiration
- **2024 Rule:** When a player rolls a natural 1 on a d20 Test, they gain **Heroic Inspiration** (a free advantage to use later on any d20 Test)
- A character can hold only **one** Heroic Inspiration at a time
- The DM can also award Heroic Inspiration for great roleplaying, clever ideas, or dramatic moments
- Heroic Inspiration replaces the 2014 "Inspiration" system

### Ending Combat Early
- If the outcome is obvious, the DM can narrate the conclusion rather than playing out every round
- Offer enemies the chance to surrender or flee when appropriate

| 10    | 64,000      | 20    | 355,000     |

### Milestone Advancement (Alternative)
- Characters level up when they achieve significant story goals — no XP tracking needed
- **Session-Based:** Level up every 1–2 sessions at low levels (1–4), every 2–3 sessions at mid-levels (5–10), every 3–4 sessions at high levels (11–20)
- **Story-Based:** Level up after completing a major quest arc, defeating a significant villain, or achieving a campaign objective
- Milestone is recommended for narrative-heavy campaigns where tracking XP is burdensome

## Quick Reference Cards

### The "Rule of Cool" Check
1. Does it make sense in the fiction? → Allow it
2. Is it fun? → Allow it with a check
3. Would it break the game? → Allow it once, then discuss

### The DM's Best Friend: "Yes, and..." / "Yes, but..."
- **"Yes, and..."** — the attempt succeeds with a bonus
- **"Yes, but..."** — the attempt succeeds with a complication
- **"No, but..."** — the attempt fails but something else happens
- Avoid flat "No" — redirect to what IS possible
