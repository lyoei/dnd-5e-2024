# Sorcerer

> *Source: Player's Handbook (2024)*

Sorcerers wield innate magic that is stamped into their being. The raw, roiling power of magic is part of them. The essential art of a Sorcerer is learning to harness and channel that innate magic, discovering new and staggering ways to unleash their power.

## Class Feature Descriptions

### Level 1: Spellcasting

You cast Sorcerer spells using Charisma as your spellcasting ability. You can use an Arcane Focus as a Spellcasting Focus.

- **Cantrips:** You know four Sorcerer cantrips of your choice. You learn another at levels 4 and 10. You can replace one cantrip whenever you gain a Sorcerer level.
- **Spell Slots:** Standard full-caster progression. You regain all expended slots on a Long Rest.
- **Prepared Spells:** You prepare Sorcerer spells (no spellbook). You can replace one spell on your list whenever you gain a Sorcerer level.

### Level 1: Innate Sorcery

As a Bonus Action, you unleash your innate magic for 1 minute, gaining:

- The spell save DC of your Sorcerer spells increases by 1.
- You have Advantage on the attack rolls of Sorcerer spells you cast.

You can use this feature twice, regaining all uses on a Long Rest.

### Level 2: Font of Magic

You gain Sorcery Points (see table). You regain all expended Sorcery Points on a Long Rest.

- **Converting Spell Slots to Sorcery Points:** Expend a spell slot to gain Sorcery Points equal to the slot's level (no action required).
- **Creating Spell Slots:** As a Bonus Action, spend Sorcery Points to create a spell slot (max level 5). Created slots vanish on Long Rest.

| Spell Slot Level | Sorcery Point Cost | Min. Sorcerer Level |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 | 2 |
| 2 | 3 | 3 |
| 3 | 5 | 5 |
| 4 | 6 | 7 |
| 5 | 7 | 9 |

### Level 2: Metamagic

You gain two Metamagic options of your choice. You can use only one Metamagic option on a spell when you cast it (unless otherwise noted). You gain two more options at Sorcerer levels 10 and 17. Whenever you gain a Sorcerer level, you can replace one option.

**Metamagic Options:**

- **Careful Spell (1 SP):** Choose a number of creatures equal to your Charisma modifier (min 1). Each chosen creature automatically succeeds on its saving throw against the spell, and it takes no damage if it would normally take half damage on a successful save.
- **Distant Spell (1 SP):** Double the range of a spell with range 5+ feet, or make a Touch spell have range 30 feet.
- **Empowered Spell (1 SP):** Reroll up to your Charisma modifier damage dice (min 1). Can be used with another Metamagic option.
- **Extended Spell (1 SP):** Double the duration of a spell with a duration of 1 minute or longer, to a max of 24 hours. If the affected spell requires Concentration, you have Advantage on any saving throw you make to maintain that Concentration.
- **Heightened Spell (2 SP):** One target of the spell has Disadvantage on saves against the spell.
- **Quickened Spell (2 SP):** Change the casting time of a spell from an action to a Bonus Action for this casting. You can't modify a spell in this way if you've already cast a level 1+ spell on the current turn, nor can you cast a level 1+ spell on this turn after modifying a spell in this way.
- **Seeking Spell (1 SP):** If you make an attack roll for a spell and miss, you can reroll the d20 and must use the new roll. Can be used with another Metamagic option.
- **Subtle Spell (1 SP):** Cast without any Verbal, Somatic, or Material components, except Material components that are consumed by the spell or that have a cost specified in the spell.
- **Transmuted Spell (1 SP):** Change the damage type of a spell to Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Poison, or Thunder.
- **Twinned Spell (1 SP):** When you cast a spell that can be cast with a higher-level spell slot to target an additional creature (such as Charm Person), you can spend 1 Sorcery Point to increase the spell's effective level by 1.

### Level 3: Sorcerer Subclass

You gain a Sorcerer subclass of your choice: Aberrant Sorcery, Clockwork Sorcery, Draconic Sorcery, or Wild Magic Sorcery.

### Level 4: Ability Score Improvement

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice. You gain this feature again at Sorcerer levels 8, 12, and 16.

### Level 5: Sorcerous Restoration

When you finish a Short Rest, you can regain expended Sorcery Points, but no more than a number equal to half your Sorcerer level (round down). Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

### Level 7: Sorcery Incarnate

If you have no uses of Innate Sorcery left, you can use it if you spend 2 Sorcery Points when you take the Bonus Action to activate it.

In addition, while your Innate Sorcery feature is active, you can use up to two of your Metamagic options on each spell you cast.

### Level 19: Epic Boon

You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat of your choice. Boon of Dimensional Travel is recommended.

### Level 20: Arcane Apotheosis

While your Innate Sorcery feature is active, you can use one Metamagic option on each of your turns without spending Sorcery Points on it.

## Subclasses

### Aberrant Sorcery

*Wield Unnatural Psionic Power*

An alien influence has wrapped its tendrils around your mind, giving you psionic power. Perhaps a psychic wind from the Astral Plane carried psionic energy to you, you were exposed to the Far Realm's warping influence, or you were implanted with a mind flayer tadpole.

#### Level 3: Psionic Spells

| Sorcerer Level | Spells |
|---|---|
| 3 | Arms of Hadar, Calm Emotions, Detect Thoughts, Dissonant Whispers, Mind Sliver |
| 5 | Hunger of Hadar, Sending |
| 7 | Evard's Black Tentacles, Summon Aberration |
| 9 | Rary's Telepathic Bond, Telekinesis |

#### Level 3: Telepathic Speech

As a Bonus Action, choose one creature you can see within 30 feet. You and the chosen creature can communicate telepathically while within a number of miles equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1 mile). To understand each other, you each must mentally use a language the other knows. The connection lasts for a number of minutes equal to your Sorcerer level. It ends early if you use this ability to connect with a different creature.

#### Level 6: Psionic Sorcery

When you cast any level 1+ spell from your Psionic Spells feature, you can cast it by spending Sorcery Points equal to the spell's level instead of a spell slot. If you do, it requires no Verbal or Somatic components, and no Material components unless they are consumed or have a cost.

#### Level 6: Psychic Defenses

You have Resistance to Psychic damage, and Advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the Charmed or Frightened condition.

#### Level 14: Revelation in Flesh

As a Bonus Action, spend 1+ Sorcery Points to magically alter your body for 10 minutes. For each point spent, choose one benefit:

- **Aquatic Adaptation.** Swim Speed equal to twice your Speed; you can breathe underwater.
- **Glistening Flight.** Fly Speed equal to your Speed; you can hover.
- **See the Invisible.** You can see any Invisible creature within 60 feet that isn't behind Total Cover.
- **Wormlike Movement.** Move through spaces as narrow as 1 inch; spend 5 feet of movement to escape nonmagical restraints or the Grappled condition.

#### Level 18: Warping Implosion

As a Magic action, teleport to an unoccupied space you can see within 120 feet. Each creature within 30 feet of the space you left must make a Strength saving throw. On a failure: 3d10 Force damage and pulled to the nearest unoccupied space to where you were. On a success: half damage only.

Once used, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest unless you spend 5 Sorcery Points to restore it.

### Clockwork Sorcery

*Channel Cosmic Forces of Order*

The cosmic force of order has suffused you with magic, arising from Mechanus or a realm like it—a plane of existence shaped entirely by clockwork efficiency.

#### Level 3: Clockwork Spells

| Sorcerer Level | Spells |
|---|---|
| 3 | Aid, Alarm, Lesser Restoration, Protection from Evil and Good |
| 5 | Dispel Magic, Protection from Energy |
| 7 | Freedom of Movement, Summon Construct |
| 9 | Greater Restoration, Wall of Force |

#### Level 3: Restore Balance

When a creature you can see within 60 feet is about to roll a d20 with Advantage or Disadvantage, you can take a Reaction to prevent the roll from being affected by Advantage and Disadvantage.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum once), regaining all uses on a Long Rest.

#### Level 6: Bastion of Law

As a Magic action, expend 1 to 5 Sorcery Points to create a magical ward around yourself or another creature you can see within 30 feet. The ward has a number of d8s equal to the Sorcery Points spent. When the warded creature takes damage, it can expend dice, roll them, and reduce the damage taken by the total.

The ward lasts until you finish a Long Rest or use this feature again.

#### Level 14: Trance of Order

As a Bonus Action, enter this state for 1 minute:

- Attack rolls against you can't benefit from Advantage.
- Whenever you make a D20 Test, you can treat a roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

Once used, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest unless you spend 5 Sorcery Points to restore it.

#### Level 18: Clockwork Cavalcade

As a Magic action, summon spirits of order in a 30-foot Cube originating from you. The spirits create the following effects before vanishing:

- **Heal.** Restore up to 100 Hit Points, divided as you choose among any number of creatures in the Cube.
- **Repair.** Any damaged objects entirely in the Cube are repaired instantly.
- **Dispel.** Every spell of level 6 and lower ends on creatures and objects of your choice in the Cube.

Once used, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest unless you spend 7 Sorcery Points to restore it.

### Draconic Sorcery

*Breathe the Magic of Dragons*

Your innate magic comes from the gift of a dragon. Perhaps an ancient dragon bequeathed some of its magical power to you or your ancestor, or you might have a dragon for an ancestor.

#### Level 3: Draconic Resilience

Your Hit Point maximum increases by 3, and it increases by 1 whenever you gain another Sorcerer level.

While you aren't wearing armor, your base AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Charisma modifier.

#### Level 3: Draconic Spells

| Sorcerer Level | Spells |
|---|---|
| 3 | Alter Self, Chromatic Orb, Command, Dragon's Breath |
| 5 | Fear, Fly |
| 7 | Arcane Eye, Charm Monster |
| 9 | Legend Lore, Summon Dragon |

#### Level 6: Elemental Affinity

Choose one damage type: Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Poison.

You have Resistance to that damage type, and when you cast a spell that deals damage of that type, you can add your Charisma modifier to one damage roll of that spell.

#### Level 14: Dragon Wings

As a Bonus Action, cause draconic wings to appear on your back. The wings last for 1 hour or until you dismiss them (no action required). For the duration, you have a Fly Speed of 60 feet.

Once used, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest unless you spend 3 Sorcery Points to restore it.

#### Level 18: Dragon Companion

You can cast Summon Dragon without a Material component. You can also cast it once without a spell slot, regaining the ability on a Long Rest.

Whenever you start casting the spell, you can modify it so that it doesn't require Concentration. If you do so, the spell's duration becomes 1 minute for that casting.

### Wild Magic Sorcery

*Unleash Chaotic Magic*

Your innate magic stems from the forces of chaos that underlie the order of creation. This magic churns within you, waiting for any outlet.

#### Level 3: Wild Magic Surge

Once per turn, you can roll 1d20 immediately after you cast a Sorcerer spell with a spell slot. If you roll a 20, roll on the Wild Magic Surge table to create a magical effect. If the effect is a spell, it is too wild to be affected by your Metamagic.

#### Level 3: Tides of Chaos

You can give yourself Advantage on one D20 Test before you roll. Once you do so, you must cast a Sorcerer spell with a spell slot or finish a Long Rest before you can use this feature again. If you cast a Sorcerer spell with a spell slot before finishing a Long Rest, you automatically roll on the Wild Magic Surge table.

#### Level 6: Bend Luck

Immediately after another creature you can see rolls the d20 for a D20 Test, you can take a Reaction and spend 1 Sorcery Point to roll 1d4 and apply the number rolled as a bonus or penalty (your choice) to the d20 roll.

#### Level 14: Controlled Chaos

Whenever you roll on the Wild Magic Surge table, you can roll twice and use either number.

#### Level 18: Tamed Surge

Immediately after you cast a Sorcerer spell with a spell slot, you can create an effect of your choice from the Wild Magic Surge table instead of rolling. You can choose any effect except the final row, and if the chosen effect involves a roll, you must make it.

Once used, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest.

## 2024 Changes (vs. 2014 PHB)

- **Innate Sorcery** is a completely new level 1 feature (+1 spell save DC, Advantage on spell attack rolls for 1 minute).
- **Sorcerers now prepare spells** rather than having "spells known." Spell list is larger and more flexible.
- **Font of Magic** and **Metamagic** both start at level 2 (previously level 3 for Metamagic in some versions).
- **Sorcery Points** equal Sorcerer level (starting at 2 at level 2) — previously started at 2 at level 2 as well, but the table is more generous in total options.
- **Sorcerous Restoration** (level 5) allows recovering Sorcery Points on a Short Rest (new feature replacing what was previously a level 20 capstone in 2014).
- **Sorcery Incarnate** (level 7) is entirely new — spend Sorcery Points to activate Innate Sorcery when out of uses, and use two Metamagic options per spell while Innate Sorcery is active.
- **Arcane Apotheosis** (level 20) is a new capstone — free Metamagic once per turn while Innate Sorcery is active.
- **Metamagic** gains options at levels 2, 10, and 17 (total of 6 options known). Twinned Spell and Seeking Spell are retained/refined.
- **Subclass features** at levels 3, 6, 14, 18 (previously 1/6/14/18 in 2014). All subclasses now start at level 3.
- **Draconic Sorcery:** Draconic Resilience AC now uses Charisma modifier in addition to Dexterity. Dragon Wings are a temporary feature (1 hour) instead of permanent. Dragon Companion (level 18) replaces Draconic Presence.
- **Wild Magic:** Wild Magic Surge trigger changed (roll 1d20, surge on 20, once per turn) instead of DM-decided. Tamed Surge replaces Spell Bombardment.
- **Aberrant Sorcery and Clockwork Sorcery** were previously in Tasha's Cauldron of Everything; now in the core PHB with updates.
- Four ASIs (levels 4, 8, 12, 16) plus Epic Boon at 19.
