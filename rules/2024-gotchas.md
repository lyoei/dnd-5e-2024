# Common Mistakes & Gotchas — D&D 5e 2024

A comprehensive guide to the most frequently misunderstood or overlooked rule changes in the 2024 PHB. Essential reading for tables transitioning from 2014 rules.

---

## 1. Character Creation Gotchas

### Ability Score Increases Come from Background, Not Species
- **2014:** Species (race) determined your ASIs (e.g., +2 Dex for Elves).
- **2024:** Your **Background** gives you +2/+1 (or +1/+1/+1) to ability scores of your choice. Species no longer grant ASIs.
- **Common mistake:** Applying racial ASIs from 2014 species entries on top of background ASIs, resulting in inflated stats.

### Origin Feats Replace Racial Feats at Level 1
- Every character gets an **Origin Feat** at level 1, chosen from: Alert, Crafter, Healer, Lucky, Magic Initiate, Musician, Savage Attacker, Skilled, Tavern Brawler, Tough.
- **Common mistake:** Thinking you still get a racial feat (like the 2014 Variant Human's free feat) on top of an Origin Feat. The Origin Feat *is* your level-1 feat.
- **Also:** Human Resourcefulness (the 2024 Human trait) grants Heroic Inspiration on a Long Rest and the Skillful trait — it does NOT grant a bonus feat.

### "Race" Is Now "Species"
- Terminology changed throughout. If using 2014 species in a 2024 game, ignore the ASIs from the old species entry and use background ASIs instead.

### Backgrounds Are Fully Customizable
- The 16 sample backgrounds are starting points. You can mix and match ASIs, skill proficiencies, tool proficiency, and Origin Feat regardless of which named background you pick.

## 2. Spellcasting Gotchas

### Spell Lists Are Now Arcane / Divine / Primal
- **2014:** Each class had its own unique spell list.
- **2024:** Three shared spell lists: **Arcane** (Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard), **Divine** (Cleric, Paladin), **Primal** (Druid, Ranger).
- Classes may add specific spells to their "always prepared" list beyond their base list.
- **Common mistake:** Looking up "Wizard spell list" — there isn't one anymore. Wizards use the Arcane list.

### Spell Preparation Changes
- Most full casters now **prepare** spells daily (including Sorcerers and Warlocks, who previously knew spells permanently).
- **Bards** now prepare from the Arcane list instead of having a fixed "spells known."
- **Common mistake:** Running Sorcerers or Bards with fixed spells known like 2014.

### One Leveled Spell Per Turn Rule — GONE (Mostly)
- **2014:** If you cast a Bonus Action spell, you could only cast a cantrip with your Action.
- **2024:** The restriction is loosened. You can cast a leveled spell as your Action AND a leveled Bonus Action spell in the same turn (e.g., Misty Step + Fireball is legal).
- **However:** The general rule is now: you can't cast two spells using spell slots on the same turn unless one is a cantrip. Check the exact Rules Glossary wording — the restriction is on *using spell slots*, not on bonus action timing.
- **Common mistake:** Either applying the old 2014 restriction too broadly, or assuming there are zero restrictions.

### Ritual Casting Reworked
- Ritual casting is now tied to the **Ritual Caster** Origin Feat or specific class features.
- Not all classes that could ritual-cast in 2014 can do so by default in 2024.

### Multi-Target Spell Damage
- Spells requiring saving throws roll damage once and apply to all targets (same as 2014).
- Spells with attack rolls (like Scorching Ray) roll damage separately per target.
- **Magic Missile:** Each dart now rolls its own 1d4+1 (no more single-roll-for-all-darts ruling).

## 3. Combat Gotchas

### Weapon Mastery Is New and Powerful
- Martial classes (Fighter, Barbarian, Paladin, Ranger, Rogue, Monk) gain access to **Weapon Mastery** properties.
- Each weapon has a mastery property (Cleave, Graze, Nick, Push, Sap, Slow, Topple, Vex).
- **Common mistake:** Forgetting to use mastery properties, or thinking they apply to all weapon users (they don't — you need the class feature).
- **Nick** (light weapons) lets you make the extra Two-Weapon Fighting attack as part of the Attack action, not as a Bonus Action.

### Two-Weapon Fighting Reworked
- The Bonus Action attack for TWF now adds your ability modifier to damage by default (no longer requires the Fighting Style).
- With the **Nick** mastery, the extra attack is part of the Attack action itself.
- **Light** property now applies to ranged weapons too (hand crossbows, etc.).

### New Actions: Influence, Study, Search
- **Influence:** Social interaction action (Charisma checks to change attitudes).
- **Study:** Intelligence-based examination (replaces ad-hoc knowledge checks).
- **Search:** Wisdom-based perception/investigation in combat.
- **Common mistake:** Not offering these as explicit action options to players.

### Exhaustion Is Now -2 Per Level (Not Stacking Penalties)
- **2014:** 6 levels with escalating, asymmetric penalties (disadvantage → speed halved → etc. → death at 6).
- **2024:** Each level of Exhaustion gives **-2 to all d20 Tests** (ability checks, attack rolls, saving throws). 10 levels = death (because -20 makes everything impossible).
- Exhaustion reduces by 1 per Long Rest.
- **Common mistake:** Using the old 6-level table or the old "speed halved" penalties.

### Surprise Is Simplified
- **2014:** Surprised creatures couldn't act in the first round.
- **2024:** Surprised creatures have **Disadvantage on Initiative rolls**. They still act normally once combat begins.
- **Common mistake:** Skipping surprised creatures' first turns entirely.

### Opportunity Attacks Changed
- Now triggers when "a creature you can see" leaves your reach (was "a hostile creature").
- Combined with War Caster, you can cast beneficial spells on allies leaving your reach.

### Grapple & Shove Are Unarmed Strikes
- Grapple and Shove are now options when you make an **Unarmed Strike**, not separate contested checks.
- Target makes a **Strength or Dexterity saving throw** (their choice) vs your Unarmed Strike DC.
- **One grapple per hand** — no more grappling multiple creatures with one hand.

### Movement Through Allies
- Moving through an ally's space is **free** (no longer difficult terrain).

### Drawing/Stowing Weapons
- You can draw or stow one weapon as part of an attack during the Attack action. The weapon doesn't need to be the one you're attacking with.

## 4. Class-Specific Gotchas

### Paladin: Divine Smite Is Now a Spell
- **2014:** Divine Smite was a class feature — free to use on any hit, no action required.
- **2024:** Divine Smite is a **1st-level spell** that must be prepared, uses a Bonus Action to cast, and requires Concentration.
- **Consequences:**
  - Can't Smite while Concentrating on another spell (e.g., Bless or Shield of Faith).
  - Can't Smite while Raging (Barbarian multiclass).
  - Only works on **melee weapon attacks** (was always melee, but now explicitly spell-based).
  - Uses your Bonus Action, competing with other Bonus Action abilities.
  - Can be Counterspelled.
- **Common mistake:** Running Smite as a free rider on weapon attacks like 2014.

### Monk: Ki → Focus Points (or Discipline Points)
- **2014:** Ki Points.
- **2024:** Renamed to **Discipline Points** (originally called Focus Points during playtest).
- Monk also gets **Martial Arts die improvements** at different levels than 2014.
- **Deflect Attacks** (formerly Deflect Missiles) now works against melee attacks too (at higher levels).
- **Stunning Strike** now occurs on hit (target makes a Con save at the *start of its next turn*) — the stun doesn't take effect until then.
- **Common mistake:** Referring to Ki Points or applying old Stunning Strike timing.

### Ranger: No More Favored Enemy / Natural Explorer
- Completely reworked. Rangers now get **Favored Enemy** as a damage feature (Hunter's Mark is always prepared and doesn't require Concentration at level 1... wait, it does initially but eventually doesn't).
- **Deft Explorer** replaces Natural Explorer with more universally useful exploration features.
- Rangers are now full prepared casters from the Primal list.
- **Common mistake:** Expecting terrain-based or creature-type-based ribbon features.

### Warlock: Pact Boon Is Now a Subclass Feature
- Pact of the Blade, Chain, Tome, etc. are no longer universal options — they're tied to subclass design.
- Warlocks now prepare spells (Arcane list) instead of knowing a fixed number.
- Mystic Arcanum still exists for 6th+ level spells.

### Fighter: Weapon Mastery Swapping
- Fighters can swap one weapon's mastery property for another when they attack. This is a *Fighter-specific* feature, not universal.

### Sorcerer: Prepares Spells Now
- No longer "Spells Known." Sorcerers prepare spells daily from the Arcane list, like Wizards.

### Druid: Wild Shape Reworked
- Wild Shape forms now use a stat block template rather than adopting a beast's full stat block.
- Circle of the Moon druids get enhanced forms, but the mechanic is fundamentally different.

## 5. Multiclass Gotchas

### Multiclass Requirements Unchanged (Mostly)
- Still need 13 in the primary stat of both your current and new class.
- **Key change:** Since Divine Smite is now a spell, a Paladin 2 / Sorcerer X no longer smites with Sorcerer spell slots as freely — Smite competes with Concentration.

### Cantrip Scaling
- Cantrips still scale with **total character level**, not class level. No change here, but still a common point of confusion.

### Extra Attack Doesn't Stack
- If you multiclass two classes that both get Extra Attack, you don't get 3 attacks. You get the best version.
- **Exception:** Fighter's Extra Attack improvements (3 attacks at 11, 4 at 20) are Fighter-specific.

### Spell Slot Calculation
- Multiclass spell slot table still uses the 2014 formula. Warlock Pact Magic slots are still separate.

### Subclass Timing
- Most classes now get their subclass at **Level 3** (standardized). Some 2014 classes got them at levels 1 or 2.
- **Common mistake:** Dipping 1 level into Cleric for a Domain — you now need 3 levels.

## 6. Common 2014 → 2024 Confusion Points

| Topic | 2014 Rule | 2024 Rule |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| ASIs | From species (race) | From background |
| Level 1 feat | Variant Human only | Everyone (Origin Feat) |
| Spell lists | Per-class | Arcane / Divine / Primal |
| Surprise | Skip first turn | Disadvantage on Initiative |
| Exhaustion | 6 levels, escalating penalties | -2 per level to d20 Tests, 10 levels |
| Divine Smite | Class feature (free on hit) | Spell (Bonus Action, Concentration) |
| Grapple/Shove | Contested Athletics checks | Unarmed Strike + Saving Throw |
| TWF bonus attack | Doesn't add ability mod | Adds ability mod by default |
| Subclass level | Varies (1, 2, or 3) | Standardized at 3 |
| Sorcerer spells | Spells Known (fixed) | Prepared (daily) |
| Species ASIs | Yes | No (use background) |
| Heroic Inspiration | DM award, use before roll | Reroll any die after rolling |
| Bloodied | Not a term in 5e | Reintroduced (≤ half HP) |
| Hide DC | Variable (DM sets) | Base DC 15 |
| Opportunity Attack | Hostile creature leaves | Creature you can see leaves |
| Ally space movement | Difficult terrain | Free movement |

## Quick Reference: What Breaks If You Mix 2014 & 2024

1. **2014 species + 2024 backgrounds:** Works fine — just ignore the 2014 species ASIs and use the background ASIs.
2. **2014 feats in 2024:** Many feats were rebalanced. Check if the 2024 version exists before using a 2014 feat.
3. **2014 subclasses in 2024 classes:** Mostly works, but watch for timing (subclass at 3 now) and feature interactions.
4. **2014 spells in 2024:** Most work fine, but check for rewording. Some spells changed significantly (e.g., Healing Spirit, Conjure spells).
