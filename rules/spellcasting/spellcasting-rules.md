# Spellcasting Rules

> Source: 2024 Player's Handbook

## Spell Level

Every spell has a level from 0 to 9. **Cantrips** are level 0 spells that can be cast at will without expending a spell slot. The higher a spell's level, the more powerful it is.

## Spell Slots

Spell slots represent a spellcaster's capacity to cast level 1+ spells.

- When you cast a spell, you **expend a slot** of that spell's level or higher.
- Finishing a **Long Rest** restores all expended spell slots.
- You can **upcast** a spell by using a higher-level slot than required. Some spells have enhanced effects when upcast.

### Spell Slot Progression

Each spellcasting class has its own spell slot progression table. **Multiclass** characters use a combined spell slot table based on their total spellcasting levels.

## Spell Preparation

### Prepared Spellcasters

Most 2024 classes are **prepared spellcasters**. When you finish a Long Rest, you choose a number of spells from your class spell list to prepare. The number of spells you can prepare typically equals your **spellcasting ability modifier + your class level** (minimum of 1).

**2024 Change:** All spellcasting classes now prepare spells from their class's spell list. The distinction between "known spells" (Sorcerer, Ranger, etc.) and "prepared spells" (Cleric, Wizard, etc.) from 2014 has been **eliminated**. All casters can now swap their prepared spells on a Long Rest.

### Always Prepared Spells

Some features grant spells that are **always prepared** and don't count against your number of prepared spells. These include:
- Subclass spells
- Species spells (e.g., Elf Lineage spells, Tiefling Legacy spells)
- Certain feats

## Spell Lists

**2024 Change:** Spell lists are reorganized. Instead of each class having a unique list, the 2024 PHB uses broader spell lists that multiple classes share:
- **Arcane** spell list (used by Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard)
- **Divine** spell list (used by Cleric, Paladin)
- **Primal** spell list (used by Druid, Ranger)

Each class has access to one of these lists, and subclasses may grant access to spells from other lists.

## Casting a Spell

### Casting Time

Most spells require the **Magic action** to cast. Others require a **Bonus Action**, a **Reaction**, or 1 minute or more.

### One Spell with a Spell Slot per Turn

On a turn, you can expend **only one spell slot** to cast a spell. This means you can't cast a leveled spell with the Magic action AND another leveled spell with a Bonus Action on the same turn.

**You CAN:**
- Cast a cantrip + a leveled spell on the same turn
- Cast a leveled spell on your turn + a Reaction spell on someone else's turn

**2024 Change:** This replaces the 2014 "Bonus Action spell" rule, which said if you cast a spell as a Bonus Action, you could only cast a cantrip with your action. The 2024 rule is simpler: one spell slot per turn, period.

### Longer Casting Times

Spells with casting times of 1 minute or more require you to take the **Magic action** on each of your turns while casting, and you must maintain **Concentration** while doing so. If your Concentration is broken, the spell fails and the spell slot is not expended.

## Components

Spells require one or more of the following:

### Verbal (V)
You must speak the spell's mystic words. You can't cast a V spell if you're silenced or in an area of Silence.

### Somatic (S)
You must have at least one free hand to perform the spell's gestures. You can use the same hand that holds a spellcasting focus.

### Material (M)
You must have the specified material components in hand (or in a component pouch / arcane focus). If a material component has a **gold cost** or is **consumed** by the spell, it must be the specific material — a focus cannot substitute for it.

**2024 Change:** Clarified that a free hand used for Somatic components can be the same hand holding a Material component or focus.

## Concentration

Some spells require **Concentration** to maintain. If you lose Concentration, the spell ends.

**You lose Concentration if:**
1. You **cast another Concentration spell** — the first one ends immediately.
2. You **take damage** — make a **Constitution saving throw** (DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher). On a failure, you lose Concentration.
3. You are **Incapacitated** or **killed**.
4. The DM decides that **environmental phenomena** (e.g., a massive wave) require a Concentration save (DC 10).

*2024 Change: Essentially unchanged from 2014.*

## Ritual Casting

A spell with the **Ritual** tag can be cast as a Ritual. The Ritual version takes **10 minutes longer** to cast but **doesn't expend a spell slot**.

To cast a spell as a Ritual, a spellcaster **must have it prepared**.

**2024 Change:** In 2014, Wizards could ritual cast any spell in their spellbook (even if not prepared). In 2024, all classes must have the spell prepared to ritual cast it.

## Schools of Magic

Each spell belongs to one of eight schools:

| School | Typical Effects |
|---|---|
| **Abjuration** | Prevents or reverses harmful effects |
| **Conjuration** | Transports creatures or objects |
| **Divination** | Reveals information |
| **Enchantment** | Influences minds |
| **Evocation** | Channels energy to create effects (often destructive) |
| **Illusion** | Deceives the mind or senses |
| **Necromancy** | Manipulates life and death |
| **Transmutation** | Transforms creatures or objects |

Schools are descriptive categories — they have no inherent rules of their own, though some features reference them.

## Casting in Armor

You must have **training** (proficiency) with any armor you are wearing to cast spells while wearing it. Otherwise, you are too hampered by the armor for spellcasting.

## Targets

- **Self:** The spell targets only you.
- **Specific Target:** The spell specifies what it targets (creature, object, etc.).
- **Area of Effect:** The spell's area determines who is affected. Common shapes: Cone, Cube, Cylinder, Emanation, Line, Sphere.

## Saving Throws

Many spells require the target to make a **saving throw**. The DC equals **8 + your spellcasting ability modifier + your Proficiency Bonus** (+ any special bonuses).

**2024 Change:** "If you don't want to resist the effect, you can choose to fail the save without rolling."

## 2024 Spellcasting Changes Summary

| Change | 2014 | 2024 |
|---|---|---|
| Spell lists | Per-class unique lists | Arcane / Divine / Primal shared lists |
| Known vs. Prepared | Some classes "know" spells (can't swap daily) | **All classes prepare** spells (swap on Long Rest) |
| Bonus Action spell rule | If BA spell, action can only be cantrip | One spell slot per turn (simpler) |
| Ritual casting | Wizard: from spellbook (even unprepared) | All classes: must be prepared |
| Cast a Spell action | "Cast a Spell" | Renamed to **Magic** action |
| Saving throw failure | Must roll | Can choose to fail voluntarily |
